Game Feature: The Legion's Shadow

To make the entire old world feel relevant, we introduce a persistent, server-wide feature: The Legion's Shadow. This system causes demonic incursions in zones with a historical Legion presence.

On a weekly cycle, one or two classic zones (e.g., Felwood, Blasted Lands, Azshara, Desolace) fall under The Legion's Shadow. Demonic lieutenants, dispatched from Outland, establish forward bases. The skybox darkens, Legion banners appear, and new, elite level 70 demonic mobs patrol the area.

This creates dynamic, emergent world content. Players can band together to assault these bases, disrupt rituals, and slay the commanding lieutenant (a world boss with a multi-day respawn timer). Success grants a server-wide buff (e.g., bonus reputation with Shattered Sun Offensive).

New Dungeons & Raids

NEW DUNGEON: The Halls of Damnation (Stratholme) üíÄ

A cabal of surviving dreadlords has returned to Stratholme, using the city's ambient necromantic energy to tear open a permanent gateway to the Twisting Nether. Kael'thas, seeking to curry favor with Kil'jaeden, has dispatched Magister Astromancer Vexil to aid them.

  • Wing 1: The Scarlet Breach: Fight through the corrupted spirits of Scarlet Crusaders, now bound in eternal service to the dreadlords they once fought.
  • Wing 2: The Sunfury Blight: The architecture shifts, warped by blood elven magic. Face Sunfury blood mages, mana-starved warlocks, and fel-infused constructs.
  • Final Boss: Lord Valerius: A classic dreadlord encounter focused on sleep, mind control, and summoning swarms of carrion bats, but with a new twist: players must use braziers of holy fire, consecrated by A'dal's light, to break his vampiric aura.

NEW RAID: The Siege of Quel'Danil (10-Player) üèπ

‚ñº

With the Sunwell being prepared for Kil'jaeden, Kael'thas moves to eliminate all rival claimants to elven magical lore. He dispatches a brutal commander, Warlord Salaris, to eradicate the high elves of Quel'Danil Lodge and seize their ancient runestones. The Wildhammer dwarves of Aerie Peak join the defense.

  • Boss 1: The Fel-Siege: Players must use Wildhammer cannons to destroy incoming fel-reaver prototypes while fending off waves of Sunfury engineers.
  • Boss 2: Grand Magister Lyandra: A powerful blood mage attempting to corrupt the main runestone. The fight involves siphoning her fel energy to prevent the stone from shattering.
  • Boss 3: The Twin Sentinels: Two legendary high elven guardians of the lodge are raised and empowered by fel magic. They must be fought apart and utilize coordinated attacks.
  • Final Boss: Warlord Salaris: A heavily armored Blood Elf warrior mounted on a massive, armored felboar. The fight has two phases: first defeating his mount, then facing him on foot as he uses brutal, fel-infused bladework.

A new island quest hub, "Azure-wreck Point," is established off the coast of Feralas. Players discover a group of waking Draenei survivors led by Artificer Zal'naar. The wreckage is attracting local naga, who are twisting the Exodar's technology with Vashj's magic, and agents of the Ethereum, who seek to plunder the arcane secrets.

  • Rescue and Recovery: Players rescue sleeping Draenei from damaged stasis pods and recover key technological components from the naga.
  • Repelling the Plunderers: The questline shifts to combating the ethereal incursions, culminating in shutting down a phase-portal they've established to steal technology.
  • The Core's Call & Dungeon Quest: Zal'naar realizes a larger shard of the Exodar's power core crashed deep within the Temple of Atal'Hakkar. Players must venture into a level 70 version of the Sunken Temple, where the leaking energy has created bizarre "arcane-troll" hybrids and empowered the Shade of Eranikus. They must retrieve the core fragment before it overloads and destroys the swamp.

Relevance: This creates a direct link between the Draenei starting experience and the rest of the world, revitalizes Feralas, and re-purposes a classic dungeon with a compelling narrative twist.

Endgame in Azeroth: Level 70 Content

The fight for Azeroth doesn't end when you step through the Dark Portal. At level 70, the old world becomes a new frontier, offering unique challenges and rewards for seasoned heroes.
